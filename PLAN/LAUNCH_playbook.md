# Launch Playbook (reusable)

**Created:** 2026-05-01
**Sources:** Amy Hoy / Slash7 — *Launch Crash Course* ("A Thousand Launches" PDF + Brennan Dunn / Brandon Savage / Chris Hartjes email-sequence examples + Amy's own *Launch Roundtable* 8-email sequence). Local copies in `~/Downloads/ebooks/Launch Crash Course/`.
**Use this for:** the W10 paid challenge, every cohort enrollment, the W30 ChainVault SaaS launch, every future product/cohort/event.

---

## 1. Core rules (in priority order)

1. **Never launch cold.** A pre-launch email sequence is non-negotiable. Single-email "doors are open" launches lose ~95% of revenue (Amy's own $100k bungled launch — only 1 sale).
2. **You don't have a Launch Day. You have a Thousand Launch Days.** Every commit, every blog post, every video, every demo screenshot = a micro-launch. The product survives because of the snowball, not the splash.
3. **It's easy to ignore one announcement. It's hard to ignore seven.** Repeat the same launch from 7 angles — Pain, Dream, Reason, Story, Numbers, Scarcity, Last Call.
4. **Don't say "We launched!". Say "You know that pain? Let me help you solve it."** Frame every email around the reader's life, not your milestone.
5. **Earn the right to pitch.** The first email in a sequence gives away pure value with no ask. Then you've earned the soft ask in email 2, the hard ask in email 3.
6. **Plan post-launch work BEFORE you launch.** The #1 cause of post-launch slumps is improvising the week after. Pre-write retro broadcasts, follow-up emails, next-week's video script before launch day.
7. **Transparency beats fake urgency.** "5 seats left, every email sells about 7" (Amy) beats "ACT NOW!!!" Real numbers earn trust. Real deadlines (with timezone respect) beat manufactured ones.
8. **Vulnerability beats victory.** A "here's what almost broke" retro email outperforms a "we crushed it" retro every time.

## 2. Pre-launch email sequence — the 7/5/3/1/0 cadence

Compressed Brandon Savage / Amy Hoy pattern. Send to your existing list in the 7 days before cart open.

| Offset | Type | Goal | Pitch level | Open hook |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Day-7** | Pure value | Earn the right to pitch | 0% — no product mention except in the P.S. | Teach one specific thing your audience struggles with. |
| **Day-5** | Story / autobiographical | Build emotional anchor | 20% — soft segue at the end | "Here's what happened when I…" — your own transformation. |
| **Day-3** | Reason + price reveal | Explain *why* + reveal subscriber-only price | 60% — full pitch but framed as "why I'm doing this" | "I'm running this because…" — the genuine motivation, not the sales angle. |
| **Day-1** | Tomorrow + reply CTA | Set exact time, invite questions | 80% — clear CTA, exact details | "Cart opens tomorrow at HH:MM. Here's exactly what you get." End: *"Hit reply with any question before you decide."* |
| **Day-0** | Available now | Convert | 100% — short, link, scarcity, deadline | "It's live. [LINK]. 24h subscriber discount. X of Y seats." |

**Cliffhanger rule:** every email except Day-0 ends pointing at the next one. *"In a few days I'll send you the [Reason / Story / Tomorrow] email. You'll learn ___."* Drives next-email open rate.

**Time-zone-aware deadlines:** if the discount is "24h", make it 32h so the EU/Asia subscribers get a fair shot. Mention it explicitly: *"I extended this to 32h so people in other timezones aren't punished for sleeping when I send the email."* Trust signal.

## 3. Sequence template — Pain / Dream / Reason

Three of the five emails above (Day-7 / Day-5 / Day-3) follow Amy's classic three-story structure.

### Pain email (Day-7)
- Open: name a specific moment of frustration the reader has had this week.
- Body: explain the pain mechanically — *why* it happens, what most people do wrong, what they should do instead.
- Pitch: none. P.S. only: *"I'm building something to fix this. More on Wed."*

### Dream email (Day-5)
- Open: an autobiographical moment — when *you* were in this pain.
- Body: the turning point, the specific thing that changed. Include numbers (your $X raise, your N-week shipping streak, etc.).
- Pitch: soft. *"What if you had the same turning point in 5 days, structured?"* No price.

### Reason email (Day-3)
- Open: the genuine reason you're running this offering. Not "to make money" — the deeper reason (eat dog food, fund OSS, build the cohort you wish existed).
- Body: full offering details + the price + subscriber-only discount.
- Pitch: hard. Cart opens [date/time]. Subscriber link goes out [date/time]. Price reverts to public on [date/time].

## 4. Listmonk welcome autoresponder spec (Brennan-style)

Auto-fires from the moment someone subscribes. Spreadsheet columns:

| Offset | Subject | Points covered | Key takeaway | Bridge to next |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 0d | Welcome + reply prompt | "I read every email while the list is small" + 1 specific reply ask | Direct access is the trade for being early | "In 24h I'll send you the lead magnet you signed up for" |
| 1d | Lead magnet delivery | The download + "how I'd use it" | Value, no ask | "In 2 days, the post that explains why I built it" |
| 3d | Origin / why I'm doing this | The thesis — why on-chain games on Antelope | Earned credibility | "Friday I'll show you the thing I shipped this week" |
| 5d | Latest build update | This week's commit + screenshot + what broke | Reader sees the live work | "Next week's deep dive will be on ___" |
| 8d | Best evergreen post | Link to the most-shared blog post | Free value, second magnet | "In a few days I'll share my OVH cost spreadsheet" |
| 12d | Second lead magnet | OVH cost spreadsheet (or current best free thing) | Magnet #2 — second sub-affirmation moment | "Coming up: the 5-day challenge in [month]" |
| 17d | Soft challenge mention | What the challenge is, who it's for, no price | Plant the seed early | "I'll write you when the date is locked" |
| 24d | Hand off to broadcast cadence | "From here you're on the regular Sunday list" | Reader knows the rhythm now | — |

**Build sequence in W01–W02.** No new subscriber should hit a 6-day silence before the next Sunday broadcast — that's your current biggest leak.

## 5. During-launch playbook (the live event week)

For a 5-day paid event (W10 challenge template):

| Day | Email purpose | Must include |
|---|---|---|
| Mon (Day-0) | "It's live" | Link + scarcity ("X of Y") + 24h subscriber discount |
| Tue | Day 1 recap + tonight's link | Live attendee count (transparent), what got shipped |
| Wed | Mid-challenge highlight + **gimmick** | Ticket-#N gets a 1:1 with you, free. One slot, ~30 min, low cost, real urgency. |
| Thu | Attendee wins + soft cohort hint | "What N built tonight" + 1-line on the cohort starting in 2 weeks |
| Fri | Last call | Subscriber price expires tonight; public price tomorrow |
| Sat | Post-event offer pitch | Pre-written. The cohort/upsell pitch. Don't write this Saturday — it goes out Saturday. |

**All 6 pre-written by the prior week.** No improvising under load.

## 6. Post-launch retro — the vulnerability email

Ship within 1 week of launch. Drafted *before* launch with template blanks.

Skeleton:
> What I expected: **___**
> What happened: **___**
> The thing that almost killed it: **___**
> What I'd do differently next time: **___**
> The next [cohort/launch] is **___** — link if you want in.

**Ship even if everything went well.** Amy's *"How I bungled a $100k launch"* email outperformed every victory email in her sequence. Find the genuine near-miss (technical glitch, someone almost didn't show, payment processor hiccup, copy that confused buyers). Tell that story.

**Don't:** brag about numbers without the near-miss. Naked victory laps train the list to skip your emails.

## 7. The "1,000 Launches" mindset — daily checklist

Every day, do at least one of these. Each one = a micro-launch. (List adapted from PDF p.10–11.)

- Ship a feature / fix / commit + tweet it
- Write a blog post about reader pain
- Send a personal email to a customer or warm lead
- Tweet about the product in a real conversation (not broadcast)
- Post a teaser screenshot / GIF
- Comment thoughtfully on a related blog or HN thread (not spam)
- Sweetly answer a reply that's been sitting in your inbox
- Ask one customer what would make them happier
- Ask one customer if they'd refer the product
- Tell one person in real life what you're building
- Invite one person to join the email list (with a real reason)
- Write to the list (broadcast or autoresponder edit)
- Offer a free chat / teleclass to a small group
- Guest post on someone else's audience

**Mindset shift:** stop calling W10 "the launch." It's micro-launch #~100 of ~1,000 over the product's life. Lower stakes, higher iteration, no single-day catastrophe.

## 8. What NOT to lift from LCC

- **"Midnight tonight!" hard scarcity on weekly broadcasts** — fine for paid-event close, erodes trust on regular newsletters.
- **Cold-calling, affiliate programs** — already in `FUTURE_MAYBE.md`.
- **Pricing-phobia warnings** ("DO NOT USE THIS OUT OF PRICING-PHOBIA, OR I WILL SMACK YOU") — your $5k cohort isn't underpriced.
- **Fake urgency / fake scarcity** — Amy's transparency rule trumps this.
- **Daily LinkedIn shorts / cross-post everything** — already constrained by the 20-hour/week cap (see `PLAN_amiheinesConsultant.md` §4).
